About Us
We have been committed to the protection of endangered plants, and we have conducted field surveys in Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region and Fujian Province, etc. We have learnt basic information about some of the endangered plants in China and their conservation status, and based on this information, we have conducted technical research and created a ‘virtual cultivation’ interactive device for the endangered plant, the Magnolia sieboldii.

Introduction
 

 
 
 Plant: up to 10 m tall Branches: branchlets of the current year slender, ca. 3 mm in diameter, light brown Leaves: leaves broadly obovate Flowers: white, fragrant, disc-shaped in full bloom, 7-10 cm in diameter
 
 

 
 
 In China it occurs in Liaoning, Anhui, Zhejiang, Jiangxi, N Fujian, and Guangxi. It grows in mountainous areas at 1600-2000 m above sea level.
 
 

 
 
 It is a valuable ornamental plant and an important germplasm resource. The wood can be used to make agricultural tools, and the flowers can be used to extract aromatic oil.
 
 
 
 
 
 Characteristics
 
 Magnolia sieboldii has a strong floral fragrance and beautiful flowers. The flowers are large, usually white or lavender, and sometimes yellowish or pink varieties. The leaves are obovate or ovate with serrated edges. The plant is a fast grower and prefers warm, moist climates and nutrient-rich soil.

 Conservation status
 
 An endangered species. Due to deforestation, habitat destruction, and weak natural regeneration, the distribution area is shrinking and plants are becoming fewer and fewer in number.

VIRTUAL CULTIVATION

The soil beneath the screen is rooted in a real-world digital experience that invites reflection on the future of nature and technology.
